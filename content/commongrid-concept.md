# Common Grid: Community-Maintained Energy Knowledge Base

**Domain:** Platform  
**Status:** Research/Early Implementation

## Summary

Expose the Context layer (Layer 1) as Common Grid — an open data project — seeded by Texture from public sources, then opened for community contributions. Positions Texture as the maintainer of shared energy infrastructure knowledge while the competitive moat remains in Layers 2–9.

## Why This Makes Sense

- **Public Data** - We're not giving away proprietary information, just our cleaning and structuring of publicly available data
- **Solves Cold Start** - Community contributions extend coverage far beyond what Texture can staff internally
- **Moat is Above Layer 1** - Defensibility lives in Layers 2–9: identity/access, device telemetry, entity graph, query/aggregation, event monitoring, reasoning, notifications, control
- **Ecosystem Gravity** - Utilities, cooperatives, manufacturers, and grid operators have natural incentives to keep their data accurate

## Strategic Positioning

Positions Texture not as another DERMS vendor but as the maintainer of critical shared infrastructure for the energy ecosystem.

Analogies:
- **OpenStreetMap** for geography
- **npm** for JavaScript packages
- **PeeringDB** for internet infrastructure
